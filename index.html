The Carbon Emissions Crisis

Understanding the facts is the first step toward meaningful action. These statistics reveal the urgency of our climate challenge.

Global Carbon Emissions (1990-2023)

65% of Emissions

Energy production and industrial processes account for over 65% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

16% Transportation

The transportation sector contributes 16% of global emissions, with road vehicles being the largest contributor.

12% Deforestation

Deforestation and land use changes contribute 12% of global emissions, destroying natural carbon sinks.

1.5°C Threshold

Scientists warn that exceeding 1.5°C of warming will trigger irreversible climate tipping points.

Solutions to Reduce Carbon Emissions

There are proven, effective solutions to reduce carbon emissions and transition to a sustainable future.

Energy Transition

Renewable Energy

Shifting to solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal energy can eliminate 70% of power sector emissions by 2030.

  • Solar power capacity increased 20x since 2010
  • Wind energy costs dropped 70% in 10 years

Sustainable Transportation

Carbon Capture Technology

Direct air capture and carbon storage can remove CO2 from the atmosphere and store it permanently.

Sustainable Agriculture

Regenerative farming practices sequester carbon in soil while improving food security and biodiversity.

Reforestation & Conservation

Protecting and restoring forests creates natural carbon sinks while preserving critical ecosystems.

Success Story

Copenhagen: Carbon Neutral by 2025

Copenhagen is on track to become the world's first carbon-neutral capital by 2025, 25 years ahead of Denmark's national target. Their comprehensive approach includes:

  • 98% of electricity from wind power
  • 50% of citizens commute by bicycle daily
  • District heating from waste incineration
  • Green building standards for all new construction

Frank Jensen

Mayor of Copenhagen

Take Action Today

Every individual action adds up to create meaningful change. Here's how you can reduce your carbon footprint today.

At Home

  • Switch to renewable energy providers
  • Install energy-efficient lighting and appliances
  • Improve home insulation and weatherization
  • Reduce water consumption and waste
  • Plant trees and create green spaces

In Transit

  • Use public transportation, bike or walk
  • Consider electric or hybrid vehicles
  • Carpool and combine errands to reduce trips
  • Opt for video conferencing over travel
  • Maintain vehicles for optimal fuel efficiency

In Consumption

  • Choose plant-rich, locally-sourced foods
  • Reduce, reuse, repair and recycle
  • Buy durable, sustainable products
  • Offset unavoidable emissions
  • Support climate-friendly businesses
